About Me

Professional Profile

I am a dedicated physician with over a decade of experience in clinical practice, medical education, and public health research. My passion lies in enhancing healthcare outcomes in Bangladesh and globally.

Currently, I am an Assistant Professor at Ad-Din Medical College Hospital, where I integrate scientific rigor with a patient-centered approach. I educate undergraduate and postgraduate students, emphasizing evidence-based medicine and community health, and am committed to shaping future healthcare professionals.

My research focuses on infectious diseases, chronic illness, vaccine response, and mental health. I have a strong publication record in peer-reviewed journals and have contributed to nationally significant studies as a principal investigator and co-author, with support from institutions like the Bangladesh Medical Research Council (BMRC).

Beyond academia, I excel at leading multidisciplinary teams, managing clinical studies, and delivering healthcare solutions with precision and empathy. I am known for my calm leadership, analytical skills, and unwavering dedication to ethical, patient-focused medicine.

I welcome discussions on shared interests, academic collaborations, or research opportunities.
